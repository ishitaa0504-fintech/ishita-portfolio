The Business Problem

A bank wants to identify which customers are likely to leave (churn) so it can take proactive retention actions. Customer attrition is a costly challenge — acquiring a new customer typically costs five to seven times more than retaining an existing one. By predicting churn before it happens, the bank can intervene with targeted offers, personalised outreach, or loyalty programs to keep valuable customers.

Dataset: Bank Customer Churn — 10,000 customers, 12 features. Source: Kaggle. Target variable: churn (binary: 1 = Churned, 0 = Stayed).

Key Findings from EDA

Class Imbalance ~80% of customers stayed while ~20% churned — the dataset is imbalanced, requiring careful evaluation beyond raw accuracy.
Age is the Strongest Predictor Churned customers tend to be older, with a positive correlation of 0.29 with churn — the highest among all features.
Germany Stands Out Germany has a significantly higher churn rate compared to France and Spain, suggesting regional factors worth investigating.
Active Members Stay Longer Active membership has a negative correlation of −0.16 with churn — engaged customers are far less likely to leave.
Higher Balances, Higher Churn Churned customers tend to have higher account balances, possibly indicating unmet financial needs or better offers elsewhere.
3 Distinct Customer Segments KMeans revealed: Cluster 0 (older, 47% churn), Cluster 1 (younger, high balance, 16% churn), Cluster 2 (younger, low balance, 13% churn).

Feature Engineering

Four new features were crafted from existing columns to capture relationships that raw data misses — improving the model's ability to detect churn signals.

balance_salary_ratio
Account balance relative to estimated salary. A high ratio may indicate financial stress or dependency on bank funds.
age_tenure_ratio
Customer age divided by tenure — captures how long they have been with the bank relative to their life stage.
products_per_tenure
Product usage intensity over time — customers who adopt fewer products over a longer tenure may be disengaged.
is_zero_balance
Binary flag: 1 if the customer holds zero account balance. Zero-balance accounts are disproportionately common among churners.

Conclusion

Random Forest outperformed all other models with 86.45% accuracy and a ROC-AUC of 0.847. Key churn drivers are age, account balance, and active membership status. KMeans clustering revealed 3 distinct customer segments with very different churn rates — enabling targeted, data-driven retention strategies for each group.
