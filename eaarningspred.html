01 — Research Foundation

The PEAD Anomaly
& Earnings Surprise

Every quarter, companies report Earnings Per Share. Analysts publish a consensus estimate beforehand. When reality diverges from expectation — a surprise is born. Markets systematically underreact to these surprises, creating a documented and exploitable drift.

The Surprise Formula
Surprise% = (Actual − Estimate) ÷ |Estimate| × 100
Surprise > +2%
BEAT → Bullish
Surprise < −2%
MISS → Bearish
Within ±2%
MEET → Muted
Post-Earnings Announcement Drift

Why Markets Underreact

PEAD, first documented by Ball & Brown (1968), is one of finance's most persistent anomalies. After a positive earnings surprise, stocks continue drifting upward for days — weeks. Institutional investors can't immediately absorb and act on new information. This project goes one step further: predicting the surprise before it occurs.

REFERENCED ANOMALY
Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD)
Ball & Brown, 1968 · Contradicts Efficient Market Hypothesis

"Can pre-announcement signals in price, volume, and earnings history predict whether a company will beat earnings estimates — and can we profit from that prediction?"

04 — Feature Engineering

Pre-Announcement
Signal Construction

10 features engineered strictly from data available before the earnings date. All lookback windows end at D−1. This is the single most important discipline in the project.

📈
IMP: 0.148
21-Day Price Return
ret_21d = P[D-1] / P[D-22] − 1

When analysts quietly upgrade EPS estimates, institutions front-run, pushing prices up. Pre-earnings drift may be a proxy for hidden upward revisions. Higher → more likely to Beat.

Momentum
⚡
IMP: 0.130
Relative Momentum
rel_mom = ret_21d(stock) − ret_21d(SPY)

If a stock dramatically outperforms the market 21 days before earnings, it signals company-specific positive catalyst — analyst upgrades, product wins, or sector tailwinds that will translate into a beat.

Relative Strength
📊
IMP: 0.152
21-Day Realized Volatility
vol_21d = std(log_ret) × √252

High pre-earnings volatility reflects market uncertainty about the outcome. Some research suggests high-uncertainty events produce larger surprises in both directions. Lower vol → more likely to Beat.

Volatility
⚖️
IMP: 0.123
Volatility Ratio
vol_ratio = σ(5d) / σ(63d)

If short-term vol is expanding vs long-term, the market is becoming specifically uncertain about the upcoming announcement. Ratio > 1.0 indicates vol expansion heading into earnings.

Uncertainty Signal
🔊
IMP: 0.130
Volume Spike
vol_spike = avg_vol(5d) / avg_vol(63d)

Unusual pre-earnings volume can signal informed institutional positioning. If well-connected analysts expect a beat, institutions may accumulate quietly, leaving a volume footprint before the announcement.

Informed Flow
🏆
IMP: 0.148
Beat Streak
beat_streak = Σ Beat(t-1, t-2) ∈ {0,1,2}

The most interpretable feature. Companies like AAPL and MSFT beat estimates quarter after quarter through conservative guidance + operational execution. A 2-quarter beat streak is a strong prior for the next beat.

05 — Validation Strategy

Walk-Forward Validation
No Look-Ahead Bias

Standard k-fold cross-validation is fundamentally wrong for time-series finance. It trains on future data to predict the past — producing fictional performance metrics.

⛔ Wrong Approach

Standard K-Fold CV

Randomly shuffles data and uses 2023 events to train a model predicting 2019 events. The model "knows the future." Performance metrics are completely fictional — this is look-ahead bias.

✅ Correct Approach

Walk-Forward Validation

Expands training window chronologically. To predict Q9, only Q1–Q8 are used for training. Every prediction is genuinely out-of-sample. Minimum 8 quarters warm-up before first prediction.

09 — Risk & Limitations

Honest Caveats

Every rigorous research paper discloses its limitations. These factors would all affect live trading performance and should be addressed before any capital deployment.

⚠️
Survivorship Bias
Universe = today's large-caps. Companies that failed between 2018–2024 are excluded. This inflates Beat rates and model performance metrics.
🔄
Retroactive Revisions
yfinance EPS estimate data can have retroactive revisions. Production use requires Bloomberg or Refinitiv for point-in-time EPS snapshots.
📉
Small Sample Size
~640 events across 25 stocks. ML ideally needs 10,000+ samples. High variance in all performance metrics — a few outlier events can shift results substantially.
💧
Liquidity Assumptions
3-day holding period assumes frictionless execution. Bid-ask spreads widen near earnings events. No slippage model — live fills will be ~0.05–0.10% worse.
🦠
COVID Regime Distortion
Q1–Q2 2020 earnings were chaotic and unpredictable. Surprise distributions during this period are not representative of normal market conditions.
📡
Data Quality
yfinance EPS estimates are approximate scraped data, not institutional-grade point-in-time consensus. Systematic biases in the data source cannot be fully audited.
📋
RESEARCH DISCLAIMER — This project is an academic and portfolio demonstration. It is not investment advice. Past performance of any backtested strategy does not guarantee future results. For production deployment, institutional-grade data (Bloomberg / Refinitiv), proper position sizing, and a live paper-trading trial of 60+ events are required before any capital allocation.

11 — Findings & Roadmap

Key Insights &
Future Directions

What the data revealed — and the clear path toward a production-grade system.

01 — SIGNAL EXISTS

The PEAD Signal is Real

Logistic Regression achieved AUC-ROC of 0.571 — moderate but meaningful signal above random (0.500). The ML strategy generated +48.6 basis points per trade over the Buy-Always baseline, confirming that pre-announcement price and volume signals carry genuine predictive content.

02 — FILTERING WORKS

Selective Confidence Pays

Filtering to predictions with >60% confidence (100 trades from 445 events) improved average return from 0.36% to 0.84% per trade — a 2.3× improvement. At threshold 0.66, only 23 trades fire but achieve 82.6% Beat precision and 1.35% avg return.

03 — SAMPLE SIZE MATTERS

The Fundamental Limit

~640 events is genuinely small for ML. All performance metrics carry high variance — a handful of extreme trades in 2020 or 2022 can shift cumulative returns significantly. The signal is there, but extracting it reliably requires 10,000+ events from 500+ stocks.

Production Roadmap

Steps to transform this research prototype into an institutional-grade system.

📡
Institutional Data Sources
Replace yfinance with Bloomberg / Refinitiv for point-in-time EPS consensus data. Eliminate retroactive revision risk.
🌐
Expand Universe
Scale from 25 to S&P 500 stocks for ~14,000 events. Dramatically better ML training and statistical reliability.
🧠
Gradient Boosting
XGBoost / LightGBM typically outperform Random Forest on tabular financial data. Add SHAP values for full explainability.
📊
Options IV Features
Implied volatility crush before earnings reflects market expectations. IV skew and term structure are powerful additional signals.
📰
Alternative Data
NLP on earnings call transcripts, satellite data, credit card spending data, and web scraping for analyst sentiment.
⚡
Long-Short Strategy
Add short positions for predicted misses. A market-neutral long-short book reduces beta exposure and improves Sharpe ratio.
